15:T241d,<h1>Controlling Search Agents to Perform Search with Noisy Observations and Probabilistic Guarantees</h1>
<p><strong>US Patent Application 20240427327A1</strong><br>
<strong>Published: December 26, 2024</strong><br>
<strong>Assignee: Mitsubishi Electric Research Laboratories, Inc.</strong></p>
<h2>Patent Overview</h2>
<p>This invention addresses a critical need in autonomous systems: deploying teams of search agents that can operate reliably in real-world environments characterized by sensor noise, uncertainty, and resource constraints while providing mathematical guarantees on performance.</p>
<h2>Core Innovation: Multi-Level Multi-Armed Bandit Search (MMBS)</h2>
<p>The patent introduces the <strong>Multi-level Multi-Armed Bandit Search (MMBS) method</strong>, a revolutionary approach that combines:</p>
<ul>
<li><strong>Path-level decision making</strong> for fuel-efficient agent coordination</li>
<li><strong>Region-level classification</strong> for precise target identification</li>
<li><strong>Probabilistic guarantees</strong> for mission-critical applications</li>
</ul>
<h3>Key Technical Problem Solved</h3>
<p>Traditional autonomous monitoring systems face fundamental limitations:</p>
<ul>
<li><strong>High economic costs</strong> from inefficient movement and sensing</li>
<li><strong>No finite-time guarantees</strong> on search performance</li>
<li><strong>Inability to handle noisy sensors</strong> from low-cost, lightweight equipment</li>
<li><strong>Lack of coordination</strong> between multiple agents under physical constraints</li>
</ul>
<h2>Patent Claims and System Architecture</h2>
<h3>1. Control System Architecture</h3>
<p>The patent claims a complete control system featuring:</p>
<ul>
<li><strong>Transceiver</strong> for wireless/wired communication with search agents</li>
<li><strong>Memory</strong> storing executable MMBS method instructions</li>
<li><strong>Processor</strong> executing iterative MMBS until termination conditions are met</li>
</ul>
<h3>2. Multi-Level Multi-Armed Bandit Search (MMBS) Method</h3>
<p>The core innovation includes two levels of decision-making:</p>
<ul>
<li><strong>Level 1</strong>: Individual probabilistic classifications of regions based on measurements</li>
<li><strong>Level 2</strong>: Path selection based on aggregated confidence bounds across regions</li>
</ul>
<h3>3. Path Planning and Constraints</h3>
<p>The system generates feasible paths that:</p>
<ul>
<li><strong>Start/end at pre-designated regions</strong> (charging stations, service stations)</li>
<li><strong>Respect fuel/energy constraints</strong> with maximum path lengths</li>
<li><strong>Avoid restricted regions</strong> (obstacles, no-fly zones)</li>
<li><strong>Cover the entire search environment</strong> systematically</li>
</ul>
<h3>4. Confidence Bound Management</h3>
<p>The system maintains and updates:</p>
<ul>
<li><strong>Upper and lower confidence bounds</strong> for each region's classification</li>
<li><strong>Concentration inequalities</strong> (e.g., Hoeffding bounds) for mathematical rigor</li>
<li><strong>Aggregation functions</strong> (sum, average, max, min) for path evaluation</li>
<li><strong>Termination criteria</strong> when all regions are classified</li>
</ul>
<h2>Patent Applications and Examples</h2>
<h3>Agricultural Applications</h3>
<ul>
<li><strong>Ready-to-harvest tree detection</strong>: Determining optimal harvesting times for orchards</li>
<li><strong>Crop health monitoring</strong>: Identifying diseased or stressed vegetation areas</li>
<li><strong>Yield estimation</strong>: Automated assessment of agricultural productivity</li>
</ul>
<h3>Search and Rescue Operations</h3>
<ul>
<li><strong>Disaster response</strong>: Finding humans trapped on rooftops after flooding</li>
<li><strong>Survivor detection</strong>: Coordinated teams searching for people in disaster zones</li>
<li><strong>Emergency resource allocation</strong>: Identifying areas requiring immediate attention</li>
</ul>
<h3>Infrastructure and Environmental Monitoring</h3>
<ul>
<li><strong>Traffic monitoring</strong>: Determining congested areas in urban environments</li>
<li><strong>Environmental assessment</strong>: Tracking pollution or environmental changes</li>
<li><strong>Wildlife monitoring</strong>: Coordinating observations across large habitats</li>
<li><strong>Disaster management</strong>: Rapid assessment of damage and resource needs</li>
</ul>
<h3>Agent Types and Platforms</h3>
<p>The patent covers diverse autonomous agents:</p>
<ul>
<li><strong>Aerial vehicles</strong>: Drones and UAVs for overhead surveillance</li>
<li><strong>Ground vehicles</strong>: Mobile robots for terrestrial exploration</li>
<li><strong>Water surface vehicles</strong>: Autonomous boats for marine applications</li>
<li><strong>Underwater vehicles</strong>: Submersibles for aquatic environments</li>
</ul>
<h2>Technical Advantages and Innovation</h2>
<h3>1. Finite-Time Guarantees</h3>
<p>Unlike traditional approaches, this system provides:</p>
<ul>
<li><strong>Upper bounds on search completion time</strong></li>
<li><strong>Upper bounds on time to identify all interesting regions</strong></li>
<li><strong>Upper bounds on economic costs during search</strong></li>
<li><strong>Probabilistic performance guarantees</strong> with user-specified confidence levels</li>
</ul>
<h3>2. Overcoming Traditional Limitations</h3>
<p>The patent addresses key problems in existing methods:</p>
<ul>
<li><strong>Branch-and-bound methods</strong>: Assumed knowledge of target probability distributions</li>
<li><strong>Collaborative sensor networks</strong>: Required Gaussian distribution assumptions</li>
<li><strong>Multi-arm bandit methods</strong>: Needed prior knowledge of total interesting regions</li>
<li><strong>Label-then-move search</strong>: Ignored online data for location decisions</li>
</ul>
<h3>3. Practical Deployment Features</h3>
<ul>
<li><strong>Low-cost sensor compatibility</strong>: Designed for noisy, lightweight sensors</li>
<li><strong>Energy efficiency</strong>: Explicit consideration of fuel/battery constraints</li>
<li><strong>Physical constraint handling</strong>: Accounts for agent dynamics and movement limitations</li>
<li><strong>Scalable coordination</strong>: Efficient operation with varying team sizes</li>
</ul>

<p>This patent provides:</p>
<ul>
<li><strong>20-year protection</strong> for the MMBS method and system architecture</li>

<li><strong>Environmental monitoring</strong> for research institutions</li>
<li><strong>Military and defense applications</strong> for autonomous surveillance</li>
</ul>
<h2>Implementation Considerations</h2>
<h3>Hardware Requirements</h3>
<ul>
<li><strong>Computing platforms</strong>: Edge devices, embedded systems, cloud integration</li>
<li><strong>Sensor suites</strong>: Cameras, LIDAR, environmental sensors, communication modules</li>

<h3>Software Architecture</h3>

</ul>
