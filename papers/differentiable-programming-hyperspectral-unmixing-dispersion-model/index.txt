15:T499,Hyperspectral unmixing is an important remote sensing task with applications including material identification and analysis. Characteristic spectral features make many pure materials identifiable from their visible-to-infrared spectra, but quantifying their presence within a mixture is a challenging task due to nonlinearities and factors of variation. We consider spectral variation from a physics-based approach and incorporate it into an end-to-end spectral unmixing algorithm via differentiable programming. The dispersion model is introduced to simulate realistic spectral variation, and an efficient method to fit the parameters is presented. This dispersion model is utilized as a generative model within an analysis-by-synthesis spectral unmixing algorithm. Additionally, we present a technique for inverse rendering using a convolutional neural network to predict parameters of the generative model to enhance performance and speed when training data is available. Results achieve state-of-the-art on both infrared and visible-to-near-infrared (VNIR) datasets, and show promise for the synergy between physics-based models and deep learning in hyperspectral unmixing.16:T1cf9,<!-- # Differentiable Programming for Hyperspectral Unmixing using a Physics-based Dispersion Model -->
<h2>Problem Overview</h2>
<p><strong>Hyperspectral unmixing</strong> is a fundamental challenge in remote sensing where the goal is to decompose mixed spectral signatures into their constituent pure materials (endmembers) and estimate their relative abundances.</p>
<h3>The Core Challenge</h3>
<p>While pure materials have characteristic spectral fingerprints across the visible-to-infrared spectrum, <strong>quantifying their presence in mixtures</strong> is difficult due to:</p>
<ul>
<li><strong>Nonlinear mixing effects</strong> beyond simple linear combinations</li>
<li><strong>Spectral variability</strong> caused by environmental and physical factors</li>
<li><strong>Complex light-matter interactions</strong> that traditional models oversimplify</li>
</ul>
<h2>Technical Innovation</h2>
<h3>1. Physics-based Dispersion Model</h3>
<h4>Modeling Spectral Variation</h4>
<ul>
<li><strong>Physical foundation</strong>: Based on how light interacts with materials at different wavelengths</li>
<li><strong>Dispersion effects</strong>: Captures wavelength-dependent refractive indices and scattering</li>
<li><strong>Realistic simulation</strong>: Models actual optical phenomena rather than empirical approximations</li>
</ul>
<h4>Generative Framework</h4>
<p>The dispersion model serves as a <strong>generative model</strong> that can:</p>
<ul>
<li>Synthesize realistic spectral signatures under varying conditions</li>
<li>Capture material-specific optical properties</li>
<li>Account for environmental factors affecting spectral measurements</li>
</ul>
<h3>2. Differentiable Programming Architecture</h3>
<h4>End-to-End Learning</h4>
<ul>
<li><strong>Automatic differentiation</strong>: Enables gradient-based optimization through the entire pipeline</li>
<li><strong>Physics-constrained learning</strong>: Respects physical laws while learning from data</li>
<li><strong>Parameter optimization</strong>: Efficiently fits dispersion model parameters</li>
</ul>
<h4>Analysis-by-Synthesis Approach</h4>
<p>Instead of direct inversion, the method:</p>
<ol>
<li><strong>Synthesizes</strong> candidate spectral mixtures using the physics model</li>
<li><strong>Compares</strong> with observed spectra</li>
<li><strong>Optimizes</strong> material abundances and model parameters jointly</li>
</ol>
<h3>3. Hybrid Neural Architecture</h3>
<h4>Inverse Rendering Network</h4>
<ul>
<li><strong>Convolutional neural network</strong> predicts dispersion model parameters</li>
<li><strong>Fast inference</strong>: Accelerates parameter estimation when training data is available</li>
<li><strong>Learned priors</strong>: Incorporates patterns from training data while maintaining physical consistency</li>
</ul>
<h4>Dual Pathway Design</h4>
<ul>
<li><strong>Physics pathway</strong>: Dispersion model ensures physical plausibility</li>
<li><strong>Learning pathway</strong>: Neural network provides speed and adaptation to data patterns</li>
<li><strong>Synergistic combination</strong>: Leverages strengths of both approaches</li>
</ul>
<h2>Algorithmic Contributions</h2>
<h3>1. Efficient Parameter Fitting</h3>
<ul>
<li><strong>Gradient-based optimization</strong> for dispersion model parameters</li>
<li><strong>Differentiable simulation</strong> of optical phenomena</li>
<li><strong>Scalable computation</strong> for high-dimensional spectral data</li>
</ul>
<h3>2. Material Abundance Estimation</h3>
<ul>
<li><strong>Joint optimization</strong> of abundances and physical parameters</li>
<li><strong>Constraint satisfaction</strong> ensuring physical validity (non-negativity, sum-to-one)</li>
<li><strong>Robust estimation</strong> under spectral variability</li>
</ul>
<h3>3. Enhanced Performance and Speed</h3>
<ul>
<li><strong>State-of-the-art accuracy</strong> on standard benchmarks</li>
<li><strong>Computational efficiency</strong> through neural network acceleration</li>
<li><strong>Generalization capability</strong> across different spectral domains</li>
</ul>
<h2>Experimental Validation</h2>
<h3>Datasets and Domains</h3>
<ul>
<li><strong>Infrared datasets</strong>: Long-wave infrared spectral unmixing</li>
<li><strong>VNIR datasets</strong>: Visible-to-near-infrared spectral analysis</li>
<li><strong>Cross-domain validation</strong>: Demonstrates versatility across spectral ranges</li>
</ul>
<h3>Performance Metrics</h3>
<ul>
<li><strong>Abundance estimation accuracy</strong>: Quantitative comparison with ground truth</li>
<li><strong>Spectral reconstruction fidelity</strong>: Quality of synthesized spectra</li>
<li><strong>Computational efficiency</strong>: Speed improvements over traditional methods</li>
</ul>
<h3>State-of-the-Art Results</h3>
<p>Achieved <strong>best-in-class performance</strong> on multiple benchmark datasets, demonstrating the effectiveness of combining physics-based modeling with deep learning.</p>
<h2>Applications and Impact</h2>
<h3>Remote Sensing Applications</h3>
<ul>
<li><strong>Material identification</strong>: Autonomous identification of surface materials</li>
<li><strong>Environmental monitoring</strong>: Tracking changes in vegetation, water, and soil composition</li>
<li><strong>Geological surveys</strong>: Mineral mapping and resource exploration</li>
<li><strong>Agricultural assessment</strong>: Crop health and yield estimation</li>
</ul>
<h3>Technological Impact</h3>
<ul>
<li><strong>Satellite imagery analysis</strong>: Enhanced processing of hyperspectral satellite data</li>
<li><strong>Drone-based surveying</strong>: Real-time analysis for precision agriculture and environmental monitoring</li>
<li><strong>Industrial inspection</strong>: Quality control in manufacturing using hyperspectral imaging</li>
</ul>
<h2>Methodological Significance</h2>
<h3>Physics-Informed Machine Learning</h3>
<p>This work exemplifies the <strong>physics-informed AI</strong> paradigm:</p>
<ul>
<li><strong>Domain knowledge integration</strong>: Incorporates optical physics into learning algorithms</li>
<li><strong>Interpretable models</strong>: Physical parameters provide scientific insights</li>
<li><strong>Robust generalization</strong>: Physics constraints improve extrapolation beyond training data</li>
</ul>
<h3>Differentiable Programming Innovation</h3>
<p>The use of differentiable programming demonstrates:</p>
<ul>
<li><strong>End-to-end optimization</strong>: Seamless integration of multiple processing stages</li>
<li><strong>Complex model training</strong>: Enables learning in sophisticated physical simulations</li>
<li><strong>Scientific computing</strong>: Bridge between traditional numerical methods and modern ML</li>
</ul>
<h2>Future Implications</h2>
<p>This framework opens several research directions:</p>
<ul>
<li><strong>Multi-modal sensing</strong>: Integration with other remote sensing modalities</li>
<li><strong>Real-time processing</strong>: Deployment on edge devices for autonomous systems</li>
<li><strong>Uncertainty quantification</strong>: Bayesian extensions for confidence estimation</li>
<li><strong>Transfer learning</strong>: Adaptation across different instruments and environments</li>
</ul>
