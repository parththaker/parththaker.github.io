About

About me

I'm an AI Research Engineer at Intuitive Surgical, where I build secure, privacy-preserving LLM systems — retrieval assistants over internal documents and code, guardrail and security analysis for LLM applications, and model-assisted tooling in developer workflows.

I completed my Ph.D. at Arizona State University with Prof. Gautam Dasarathy, working at the intersection of nonconvex optimization, graph theory, and multi-armed bandits — from sample-complexity and landscape analysis for quadratic feasibility to graph-informed pure exploration (GRUB, NeurIPS 2022). I care about results that are both provable and usable, and I try to carry that discipline into the systems I ship.

Selected publications

NeurIPS2022

Maximizing and Satisficing in Multi-armed Bandits with Graph Information

P. K. Thaker, M. Malu, N. Rao, G. Dasarathy

A pure-exploration bandit method (GRUB) that uses a similarity graph over arms to find the best arm — or one above a threshold — with far fewer samples than standard UCB, with a matching lower bound.

ICASSP2024

Non-Stationary Bandits with Periodic Behavior via Ramanujan Periodicity Transforms

P. Thaker, V. Gattani, V. Tirukkonda, P. Saidi, G. Dasarathy

Detects and exploits periodic structure in shifting rewards — modeling the period directly instead of re-learning the best arm after every change.

IFAC2023

Bandit-based Multi-agent Search under Noisy Observations

P. Thaker, S. Di Cairano, A. P. Vinod

A thresholding multi-armed bandit for coordinating search agents under noisy sensors, with finite-time guarantees on search time and cost. Work from a MERL internship; led to a granted US patent.

ECCV2020

Differentiable Programming for Hyperspectral Unmixing using a Physics-based Dispersion Model

J. Janiczek, P. Thaker, G. Dasarathy, C. Edwards, P. Christensen, S. Jayasuriya

Makes a physics-based dispersion model differentiable and uses it for end-to-end hyperspectral unmixing, jointly estimating abundances and physical parameters.

P. K. Thaker, G. Dasarathy, A. Nedić

Identifiability and a benign nonconvex landscape for recovering a vector from quadratic measurements — gradient descent converges globally from any initialization.
